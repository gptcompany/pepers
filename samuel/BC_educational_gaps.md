# Educational & Strategic Gaps: BC Porto Pilot (2026)
**Status:** Internal hypothesis-testing memo
**Use:** Consensus input for academic, pedagogical, and operating-model review

## 1. Core Question
Can the proposed `Stealth Math` curriculum solve a real educational and strategic gap for BC Porto, or does it create a new barrier through excessive cognitive load?

## 2. Educational Gaps

### 2.1 Learner Engagement Gap
The working assumption is that a purely textbook-led grammar model may not provide enough intellectual differentiation for secondary learners who respond well to challenge, pattern recognition, and structured problem-solving.

**Pilot hypothesis:** A logic-based approach to syntax may increase engagement because students are asked to understand transformation rules, not merely reproduce forms.

### 2.2 Meaning-Making Gap
Many grammar activities are experienced as procedural rather than conceptually rich. The proposed model attempts to reposition grammar as a system of relations, transformations, and constraints.

**Pilot hypothesis:** If students perceive English as a structured system, retention and transfer may improve.

### 2.3 Cognitive Load Risk
The same feature that makes the proposal distinctive also creates risk. Abstract algebraic framing may exceed the comfort range of some B2/C1 learners if the progression is too steep or the metaphors are poorly controlled.

**Validation requirement:** The curriculum must demonstrate that mathematical structure acts as a scaffold, not as an additional layer of confusion.

## 3. Teacher and Capability Gaps

### 3.1 Teacher Utilisation Gap
A standardised delivery model can underuse teachers who are capable of higher-value academic design and facilitation.

**Strategic implication:** If the pilot succeeds, BC Porto can reposition selected teachers not only as classroom deliverers, but as contributors to differentiated academic product design.

### 3.2 Enablement Gap
This model cannot depend on individual brilliance alone. It requires teachable routines, stable lesson assets, and clear facilitation guidance.

**Validation requirement:** The pilot must prove that the approach is repeatable by trained teachers, not only by the original designer.

## 4. Material and Product Gaps

### 4.1 Material Gap
There is no obvious off-the-shelf product in the current local portfolio that combines abstract mathematical structure with English syntax in a BC-compatible classroom format.

**Project response:** Build a controlled asset set that includes lesson sequences, task models, examples, and assessment criteria.

### 4.2 Product Definition Gap
An interesting lesson is not yet a scalable product. The proposal needs a clear definition in operating-model terms.

Required product elements:
- Value proposition for Partner Schools
- Delivery model for classroom rollout
- Teacher enablement package
- Quality assurance and content sign-off
- Pilot-to-scale decision criteria

## 5. Operating Model Gaps

### 5.1 TOM Fit
The proposal must fit a Partner School model that prioritises repeatability, academic credibility, and controlled quality.

**Validation requirement:** The pilot must show that the offer can be standardised enough for BC governance while remaining distinctive enough to justify premium positioning.

### 5.2 Evidence Gap
The project currently has a strong conceptual narrative, but it still needs operational evidence.

Required evidence categories:
- Learner engagement
- Teacher usability
- Classroom feasibility
- Assessment coherence
- School-facing differentiation value

## 6. Primary Consensus Mandate
Before any move beyond pilot stage, the consensus review should answer four questions:

1. Does `Stealth Math` reduce or increase cognitive load for the intended learner group?
2. Can the materials be produced and maintained at acceptable cost and effort?
3. Does the model fit BC Porto's quality, governance, and Partner School delivery requirements?
4. Is the educational distinctiveness strong enough to function as a genuine premium proposition rather than a one-off innovation exercise?

## 7. Decision Rule
Proceed only if the pilot demonstrates all of the following:
- Learners can access the content without conceptual overload.
- Teachers can deliver the model with limited additional complexity.
- The materials can be quality-controlled and reused.
- The offer strengthens BC Porto's academic value proposition in a credible way.
