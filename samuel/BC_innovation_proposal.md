# British Council Strategic Innovation: The Logic of Language

## 1. Strategic Case
This proposal defines a differentiated academic offer for BC Porto within the 2026 Partner School portfolio. The product combines symbolic logic and introductory abstract algebra with advanced English syntax in order to create a premium enrichment pathway for learners aged 11-19.

The operating model rationale is straightforward: the offer moves British Council Porto away from a purely textbook-led proposition and toward a high-value academic enrichment model that schools cannot easily replicate in-house.

## 2. TOM Alignment
- **Value Proposition:** A premium English-learning product that develops language accuracy, analytical reasoning, and structured problem-solving in a single classroom experience.
- **Customer Segment:** Partner Schools seeking academically distinctive English provision for high-potential secondary learners.
- **Delivery Model:** BC-led pilot delivered through the Partner School only model, supported by modular lesson assets, teacher guidance, and a repeatable facilitation sequence.
- **Capability Stack:** Academic content design, CLIL-style classroom delivery, quality-controlled AI-assisted content generation, and assessment design.
- **Performance Logic:** The pilot should improve partner-school differentiation, student engagement, and progression into higher-value British Council learning pathways.

## 3. Product Definition
Working title: **The Logic of Language**

Core learning proposition:
- Students study English as a structured system rather than as a list of isolated rules.
- Mathematical ideas such as symmetry, transformation, and structure-preservation become vehicles for deeper language awareness.
- English grammar topics such as passive voice, conditionals, and word order become more memorable because they are taught through explicit transformation rules.

## 4. Delivery Architecture
- **Pilot Format:** 4-6 session enrichment module for Partner Schools.
- **Classroom Model:** BC academic lead or trained teacher-facilitator delivers the module using a standard lesson sequence.
- **Teacher Enablement:** Short implementation guide, model activities, and assessment rubrics.
- **Quality Assurance:** Central review of all AI-assisted materials before classroom use.

## 5. Pilot-to-Scale Pathway
- **Phase 1: Pilot validation.** Test the module in a small number of partner-school classrooms and capture learner response, teacher feedback, and operational feasibility.
- **Phase 2: Product refinement.** Adjust the lesson sequence, difficulty gradient, and assessment materials using pilot evidence.
- **Phase 3: Cluster replication.** Roll out a standard package that can be reused across comparable schools in the cluster with limited redesign effort.

## 6. Governance and Success Measures
- **Academic Governance:** Content sign-off by the local academic lead before pilot deployment.
- **Delivery Governance:** Clear ownership for content, teacher briefing, scheduling, and post-pilot evaluation.
- **Success Measures:** School uptake, learner completion, teacher satisfaction, evidence of improved engagement with advanced grammar, and potential conversion into follow-on premium programmes.

## 7. Why BC Porto
BC Porto is well positioned to lead this pilot because the city already carries strong academic credibility and a visible mathematics culture. By associating the project with locally rooted research prestige, including the intellectual profile of Prof. Samuel A. Lopes, the centre can present the programme as a serious academic innovation rather than a novelty workshop.

This makes the proposal attractive at cluster level: it is distinctive, scalable, academically credible, and aligned with a Partner School delivery model that rewards differentiated value creation.
